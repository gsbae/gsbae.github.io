Bun Bun Bakeshop

A responsive e-commerce website for a bakery

Goal:

This project was an introductory project to HTML, CSS, and Javascript without the use of any additional frameworks or libraries. The goal of this project was to create an e-commerce website for a small. bakery, Bun Bun Bakeshop where customers could browse and shop for their specialty cinnamon buns.

I wanted this website to contain the traditional home page, product browsing page, product detail page, shopping cart, and about us page. While doing so, I also wanted to create a cozy environment for the customers on the website to relate the experience to buying these products in person as much as possible. I used a warm and neutral color palette and tried to be as personal as a business can be.

LoFi Prototypes

To begin this project, I started out by making paper prototypes to form a general idea of what I wanted my website to look like. I found inspiration from traditional e-commerce website in terms of the general layout of this website in order to help users feel familiar with the process of ordering online. I started with a couple of iterations of rough sketches which developed to a lofi prototype of my website, and I conducted informal user studies.

HiFi Prototypes

After establishing my design, I moved on to prototyping the screens and interactions in Figma and Invision. In addition to the prototypes, I created a site map, showing how all the screens in my protoypes linked together for future development
