Autonomous Vehicle CUI

Designed conversation for passenger and voice user interface of autonomous vehicle service

Goal:

This project was focused on designing a Conversation User Interface (CUI) that interacts with passengers in an autonomous vehicle in a way that responds with intention and context to its current location.

Iteration 1

Setting the context

After multiple brainstorming and iterations, we set on a single passenger in a fully autonomous vehicle traveling from LAX airport to the Disneyland Resort to join her family for a holiday vacation which would result in an hour car ride.

External state
  • As always, traffic in Los Angeles is touch-and-go regardless of the time of day.
  • The roads are occupied by working professionals, families, and people just driving around down going about their business.
  • Weather is relatively nice in LA, so no extreme weather conditions
Internal state
  • Emotional state
    • Excited but tired
    • Bored in the car ride
  • Physical state
    • Exhausted from flight
    • Hungry
  • Cognitive state
    • Anxious to get to the hotel
    • Wanting to relax after long flight
Initial Design
Feedback and Reflection

We learned that transactional conversations that lack “turns” are not recommended because there is little room for negotiation in the conversation. People will not receive any insights from the conversation.

We need to think about a real person (not ourselves), imagining his/her values, goals, physical or mental states, personality, and how these elements can cause certain feelings during the trip.

It is important to think about the ongoing scenario or experience: what will the passenger see/feel/hear?

Iteration 2

Value Analysis

Taking our first iteration of scenarios and scripts, we then rewrote the scripts to be controlling, delegating, guiding, and collaborating from either the passenger or the CUI’s standpoint.

From our value analysis, we planned to then create a specific persona being a grandma. We also realized we need to be more time-sensitive and context-aware.

Persona
Final Scenario

Maria is a 65-year-old lady. She has 1 son and 2 daughters. She is flying from Washington to LA to have a family trip with her youngest daughter, Natasha, and Natasha’s family - husband Travis, and 5 year old daughter, Michelle. Her daughter told her that one stop of their family trip is Disneyland. Maria has not been to LA for 15 years, and although she lived there for some time, she is unaware of the changes that may have occurred in LA. After she lands at the airport, the autonomous vehicle her daughter reserved comes to take her from the airport to the hotel where the rest of the family members are staying. It is her first time in an autonomous vehicle as well.

Design 2

Final Iteration

Final Topic
  • Conversation 1: Help Maria learn about CUI
  • Conversation 2: Guide bored Maria to listen to music
  • Conversation 3: Conversation about family and work-life balance
  • Conversation 4: Things to do with friends and family
Final Refinement
  • Conversation 1: Give shorter turns to decrease cognitive and auditory load
  • Conversation 2: Refine to connect better with other people’s script
  • Conversation 3: Improve wording by reducing ’no’ or ‘not’; expand the conversation
  • Conversation 4: Give less information at once to decrease cognitive and auditory load
Final Design
